Our Product


We created our product to revolutionize how people discern real from fake news using AI. We understand the challenges faced by individuals in navigating a landscape filled with misinformation. By leveraging AI and data science, we aim to empower users with accurate and timely detection of fake articles, thereby streamlining their access to truthful information and ensuring confidence in what they read. Our goal is to create a transformative tool that enhances efficiency and sets new standards for transparency and reliability in media consumption.

Our website will accept newspaper articles and output whether they are real or fake, integrating various types of visualizations for comprehensive analysis and providing clear insights into news credibility. We are dedicated to creating a user-friendly website for easy navigation and accessibility. We hope that our project will significantly enhance information verification capabilities for all users.


Why Our Model

For our project, we chose a Logistic Regression Model to best suit our AI. After training on credible data, this model yielded the best results in being able to accurately predict whether an article is fake or real. Logistic Regression is a statistical method used for predicting a binary outcome. In our case, we needed our AI to output a binary outcome: Real or Fake.

The Problem

Everyday, a greater number of "fake" or satirical news is created and it is getting hard to differentiate between real and fake news. We believe people should have access to news while also having confidence in its credibility, so we have created a model to predict whether a specific news/article is fake or true.

Timeline

  • Picking a Dataset

    We sourced our dataset from the Kaggle website. Picking a reliable dataset with relevant and high-quality data is crucial for the success of any machine learning project.

  • EDA

    We used many techiniques to clean and analyze our dataset.Techniques such as lemmetization, vectorization, and TF-IDF were used to clean and adjust our dataset to help the accuracy of our model.

  • Training and Testing

    After we had picked our model, we trained it with high-quality data to find the right the balance, doing our best to avoid issues such as overfitting or underfitting. This approach ensured that our model not only learned effectively from the data but also generalized well to new, unseen examples. This practice led us to produce reccomendations regarding the integrity of an article.

  • Buidling our Website

    We worked to create a website to host our model and provide a user-friendly interface. Our goal is to deliver results with a minimum accuracy of 78%, to ensure reliable information is delivered to our users.
